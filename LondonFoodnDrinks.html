Food & Drinks



London Lifestyle

The London dining scene caters for all tastes, from traditional cooked breakfast through to dinner via a decadent afternoon tea. Londoners dine relatively early, sitting down for supper at around 7:30 PM.

Craft beer by local and global breweries are part of an artisanal food and drink trend, an influence tat can be seen on restaurant and gastropub menus across the city. Typical London nightlife can include a couple of pints in a neighbourhood pub, or for a livelier evening out, clubbing in town.

Local Specialities

Atraditional full English breakfast includes eggs, sausages, baked beans toast, grilled tomatoes and black pudding (a type of blood sausage).

Fish and chips can be found at traditional "chip shops" or "chippie", where fried fish is served with chunky chips, salt and vinegar and sides like mushy peas.

Afternoon tea, often served in posh hotels, consists of a spread of scones, clotted cream and jam, cakes and delicate sandwiches. The upmarket department store Fortnum & Mason has its own tea lounge and is a popular choice for fine tea and food-related gifts.

Night Life

Neighborhood pubs, where you can go in for a pint of ale, are a beloved British institution. General opening hours are noon-11 PM, although many pubs now stay open later.

For a more of party atmosphere, head to Soho, home to dance clubs and a thriving gay and lesbian scene.

Shoreditch, Hoxton and Dalston in East London are where you'll find trendy cocktail bars and hip clubs. Camden Town is a live music mecca with a more alternative vibe.
