Tower of London

4.6
32578 reviews

The Tower of London, officially Her Majesty's Royal Palce and Fortress of the Tower of London, is a historic castle located on the north bank of the River Thames in Central London. It lies within the

London Borough of Tower Hamlets, seperated from the easter edge of the square mile of the City of London by the open space known as Tower Hill. It was founded towards the end of 1066 as part of the Norman Conquest of England. The White Tower, which gives the entire castle its name, was built by William the Conqueror in 1078 and was resented symbol of oppresion, inflicted upon London by the new ruling elite. The castle was used as a prisn from 1100 until 1952, although that was not its primary purpose. A grand palace early in its history, it served as a royal residence. As a while, the Tower is a complex of several buildings set within two concentric rings of defensive walls and a moat. There were several phases of expansion, mainly under Kings Richard I, Henry III, and Edward I in the 12th and 13th centuries. The general layout establish by the late 13th century remains despite later activity on the site.

The Tower of London has played a prominent role in English history.
