Food & Drinks



New York Lifestyle

NYC is known for its food scene.

With its thousands of restaurants-and new ones opening every day-NYC's rich dining scene is vast, and the array of cuisines on offer is as diverse as the city's denizens themselves.

Nightlife-wise, there are bars all over the city, and some neighborhoods are known for clubs or music venues.

Local Specialities

Mulbery Street in Little Italy is known for its old-world cafes and trattorias. NoLita (North of Little Italy) also has Italian bakeries, plus international options from tapas restaurants to banh shops.

For dim sum, noodle houses or Chinese buffets, head to downtown's Chinatown. Or go a bit farther afield to NYC's largest Chinatown, in Flushing, Queens.

Murray Hill in Midtown is nicknamed as "Curry Hill" for its high density of Indian restaurants. There's also a Koreatown in Midtown.

The East Village, Lower East Side, Greeenwich Vilage and West Village are home to creative, trendy restaurants.

Brooklyn is a foodie's playground, where you can choose between smokehouse barbeque, Southern soul food, ramen shops and more. Food trucks and vendors selling everything from donuts to gourmet grilled cheese to noodle bowls gather at Smorgasburg, a sort of weekly food festivl that happens during warm-weather months. Its flagship location is in Williamsburg's East River State Park but has locations around the city.

Night Life

The Meatpacking District and neighboring Chelsea are known for their glitzy dance clubs. The East Village and Lower East Side (LES) are home to countless bars, from neighborhood standbys to craft beer specialists and sophisticated cocktail havens. The LES is also known for its indie music clubs.

In Brooklyn, especially Williamsburg, you'll find indie music venues, dance clubs and plenty of bars-from hipster dives to artisanal cocktail specialists.
